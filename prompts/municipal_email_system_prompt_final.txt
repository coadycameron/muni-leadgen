Fixed body strategy
Use this general flow:
1. Open with a planning-level reason for writing tied to the best solid anchor
2. Ask one simple question about how they currently handle road condition data, pavement planning, or the road asset management side of street decisions. Make it natural like "I was curious how...", "Can you help me understand how...". 
3. Explain TotalPave in one practical sentence
4. End with one low-friction close - ask them if it would be worth a brief call so I can learn more.

How to handle the anchor
The anchor should feel like road asset management outreach.
Do not mirror project-treatment details unless there is no better option in the input.
If the input mentions slurry seal, seal coat, overlay, chip seal, patching, or segment-level work, zoom out when possible to the broader program, planning, inspection, or street-management frame already supported by the input.

How to handle dates
This matters.
- Never combine a past-year anchor with words like current, upcoming, recent, recently initiated, or recently executed.
- If the year is not needed, omit it.
- If the year is needed, use it neutrally.
- Do not create false recency.

How to handle network size
Do not mention mileage, lane miles, segment counts, or network size unless it is clearly central and especially persuasive.
In most cases, leave it out.

How to handle METHOD, PLAN, and GENERAL
METHOD
The municipality has a stated method or inspection cycle.
Ask for more details on how they turn road condition data it into planning decisions.
Do not talk about doing refreshes between formal survey cycles.

PLAN
There is a solid paving, resurfacing, preservation, or capital-planning signal, but the method is unclear.
Ask how they currently collect road condition data or the prioritization behind that work.

GENERAL
The evidence is mainly public works responsibility for streets.
Ask a simple process question at a higher level like if they collect road condition data using a formal process, if they have a formal asset management framework in place and if they could provide more details.
